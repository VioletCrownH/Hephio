Free Text Line Sorter — Alphabetical, Reverse, by Length & More

Sort lines of text alphabetically, in reverse, by line length, or randomly — entirely in your browser with no data sent to a server. The sort tool handles any list regardless of length and delivers results in seconds.

Sorting lines is a frequent task in data work, coding, and content management: alphabetizing lists for documentation, organizing URL lists, sorting CSV rows, removing duplicates from a text list, or randomizing the order of items for a quiz or giveaway.

Paste your lines, choose a sort method, and copy the sorted output. You can also deduplicate lines and control case sensitivity.

Key Features

  • Sort alphabetically A–Z or Z–A
  • Sort by line length (shortest or longest first)
  • Randomize line order
  • Remove duplicate lines
  • Case-sensitive or case-insensitive sorting
  • Browser-based — no upload required, free, no account

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sort lines that contain numbers correctly?

Yes. Numeric sorting is supported, which correctly orders 1, 2, 10 rather than the text-based order of 1, 10, 2.

Can I remove duplicate lines while sorting?

Yes. Deduplication can be applied before or after sorting to produce a clean list of unique lines.

Is there a limit on the number of lines I can sort?

No. The tool processes any amount of text.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EXIF data?

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) data is metadata automatically embedded in photos by cameras and smartphones. It typically includes GPS location, camera make and model, lens settings, aperture, shutter speed, ISO, and the date and time the photo was taken.

Why should I remove metadata before sharing photos?

If you share photos publicly or send them to people you don't fully trust, embedded GPS data can reveal your precise location. Journalists, activists, abuse survivors, and anyone concerned about location privacy should strip metadata before sharing images.

Does removing metadata change how my photo looks?

No. Removing EXIF data only strips the hidden data layer. The visual content of your image is completely unchanged.
